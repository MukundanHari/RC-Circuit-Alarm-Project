This RC Circuit alarm is my first ever electrical engineering project. Though it only ended with a breadboard finalization, I believe 
these projects are stepping stones to continously improve my skills. This project was done with no guidance which I believe aided 
my research skills. 

Beginning with the idealization, I thought of the RC circuit, and believed that the charging behaviour with the 
voltage across the capacitor was a method for me to trigger something depending on an external aspect, being a voltage divider.

Eventually, the idea evolved into an alarm that could be triggered depending on the voltage out of a voltage divider using a potentiometer,
with an LCD and MCU to see the actual expected and observed time with a certain potentiometer setting, as well as the status for the timer, 
be it ready, timing or done.

Overall, I've learnt a lot about basic circuit analysis, as well as electronic idealization with this project, which I continue into my future 
projects like my Arduino Protein Shaker, and my Rubik's Cube Solve Reconstruction Project with Raspberry Pi Hardware.
